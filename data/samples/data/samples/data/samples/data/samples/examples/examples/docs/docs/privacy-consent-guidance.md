# Privacy & Farmer Consent Guidance

AI systems supporting agriculture must respect farmer rights, privacy, and data ownership.

---

## 1) Data Minimization Principle
Collect only data required for the specific service.

Examples:
- ✔ Farm location at county or ward level
- ✔ Crop type and soil measurements
- ✖ Unnecessary personal identity information
- ✖ Sensitive household financial details unless essential

---

## 2) Consent Requirements

Farmers should understand:

- What data is collected
- Why it is collected
- How it will be used
- Who will access the data
- How long data will be stored

Consent should be:
- Clear
- Voluntary
- Revocable

---

## 3) Suggested Consent Script (Field Use)

"Your farm data will be used to provide crop and climate advice.  
Your data will not be sold or shared without permission.  
You can request deletion or stop participation at any time."

---

## 4) Data Ownership Principles
- Farmers retain ownership of their farm data
- Organizations act as data stewards, not owners
- Farmers must be able to access their data

---

## 5) Data Protection Practices
- Store data securely
- Limit access to authorized staff
- Use anonymization when sharing research datasets
- Maintain audit logs for data access

---

## 6) Data Retention & Deletion
Define:
- Maximum retention period
- Deletion request process
- Backup data deletion process

---

## 7) Risk Awareness
Potential risks include:
- Exposure of farm productivity data
- Misuse of location information
- Commercial exploitation of farmer insights

Mitigation:
- Aggregated reporting
- Controlled data sharing
- Clear governance oversight
